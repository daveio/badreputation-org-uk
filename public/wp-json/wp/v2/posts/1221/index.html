25th November is the International Day for the Elimination of Violence Against Women<\/a>.<\/p>\n

    \n
  • Asking to be raped? Not Ever<\/a>. Rape Crisis Scotland’s PSA on victim-blaming aims to change prejudicial attitudes. You can also download the poster campaign here<\/a>.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n
      \n
    • The White Ribbon Campaign<\/a>: men working to end violence against women. Show your support and pledge, or get involved in one of the listed events<\/a>.<\/li>\n
    • UNIFEM’s annual 16 Days of Activism against Gender Violence<\/a> (25th Nov – 10th Dec) details international observances and campaigns. The actions and campaigns are self-documented on the Say No – UNiTE website<\/a>. All activists are encouraged to write an account of their participation in the campaign.<\/li>\n
    • Govan and Craigton Integration Network has a full schedule of events<\/a>, focusing on the rights of women seeking asylum as a result of gender violence.<\/li>\n
    • The Department of Health is launching an information campaign<\/a>, with an information pack, posters and testimonials available.<\/li>\n
    • Support Refuge<\/a>, the domestic abuse charity, by fundraising, donating or volunteering a few hours a month.<\/li>\n
    • Support Amnesty International’s Stop Violence Against Women<\/a> campaign, and join the Women’s Action Network<\/a>.<\/li>\n
    • The joint website<\/a> of the Women Against Rape and Black Women’s Rape Action Project details UK-specific campaigns to protect women and girls, including asylum seekers, from all forms of gender violence.<\/li>\n
    • Watch the #16days<\/a> tag on twitter for local and international events, and calls to action.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n
