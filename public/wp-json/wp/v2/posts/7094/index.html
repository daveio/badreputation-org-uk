Hey everybody! TFI Friday, as usual. Have some links.<\/p>\n

    \n
  • Jenni, Rhian and I just got back from a great evening in Vauxhall – we went to see queer feminist burlesque collective Lashings of Ginger Beer Time<\/a>, so they’re our first link. Dazzlingly articulate and laugh-out-loud, they’re a bit of a must-see. Sally Outen<\/a> was barnstorming. Go and see them. You will not regret it.<\/li>\n
  • Raina Lipsitz in a compelling piece for The Atlantic – “Thelma and Louise: The last great film about women”<\/a>. Whatever your stance on T&L (and Team BadRep is roughly split squarely down the middle on it, I think!), whither progress since 1992?<\/em> is still a very damn pertinent question.<\/li>\n
  • Geek Feminism has a thoughtful article<\/a> on ‘geek girls’, objectification, and comic-con dynamics.<\/li>\n
  • Awkward Black Girl<\/a> is an excellent web series produced by film maker, actor and writer Issa Rae. It’s made enough of a splash on Kickstarter for more eps to be made: woo! (You can read Issa’s piece on that here<\/a>.) Should you need persuading further, here is how we heard about it: Lori on Feministing telling you more about it<\/a>.<\/li>\n
  • Finally, a daydream with a point<\/a> from Stavvers. Read it! It’s beautifully written, and contains a swearing, time-travelling Mary Wollstonecraft. Who, yes, is absolutely still hugely bloody relevant. If you look up there to the top right of your screen, you’ll see her words are a permanent fixture on our site design. Damn straight.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n
