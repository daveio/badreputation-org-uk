I’ve been asking the team what, if they had to build a sort of Feminist Christmas Grotto, would be under the Christmas tree, in the stocking, or just piled up in a flurry of glitter. Here’s Hannah’s tinsel-decked array of feminist Christmas stocking picks!<\/p>\n

<\/a><\/p>\n

She sez:<\/p>\n

    \n
  • “Christina Rossetti<\/strong><\/a> for In the Bleak Midwinter<\/strong>, one of the only Christmas carols I can stand ’cause the lyrics rhyme and scan and crazy shit like that.<\/li>\n
  • Patti Smith<\/strong><\/a> ’cause she’s the village wise-woman of my head and I love her.<\/li>\n
  • Cordelia Fine’s<\/strong> Delusions of Gender<\/strong><\/a> is one of the best books I’ve read and should be required reading for, y’know, everyone.<\/li>\n
  • I love Margaret Atwood<\/strong><\/a>. That is all.<\/li>\n
  • Angela Carter<\/strong> invokes lots of pastoral snowscapes in The Bloody Chamber<\/strong><\/a>. That’s kind of Christmassy, right?<\/li>\n
  • The Second Sex<\/strong><\/a> is on my good intentions list. I absolutely will read it next year.<\/li>\n
  • Introducing Post-Feminism<\/strong><\/a> is, I think, pretty flawed in its definitions, but it does do a good run-through of a lot of history.<\/li>\n
  • And Herland<\/strong><\/a> by Charlotte Perkins Gilman is a book I initially loved, which gets creepier on every re-read, but hey – it’s a 1915 SF pacifist gynotopia.”<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    Hopefully we’ll have some more Team Christmas Trees up before we close for the holiday season, but either way have a great week!<\/p>\n
