I’d really like to know how well this campaign worked for Volvo, though I assume that’s the sort of stuff we mortals will never get to see. At the time I thought it was a hilariously misconceived marketing strategy, since surely nobody is *ever* going to think a Volvo is a sexy and exciting car, and teenage girls don’t buy all that many cars (even in America?). But now I wonder if it might in fact have been brilliant, since several women in their 40sish also watch Twilight (I believe?), and they might now be inclined to buy a slightly-sexier Volvo for their family transportation needs, instead of a different boring car.

Disclaimer: I have many varied, complex and strongly-held opinions on the Twilight saga, but of course I’ve never watched any of the films nor read any of the books.
